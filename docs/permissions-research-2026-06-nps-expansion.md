# NPS Gathering Rules — June 2026 National-Park Completion Pass

Completes the national-park half of the permissions system. The prior passes
(see `permissions-research-2026-06.md`) encoded 17 `NPS_GATHERING_RULES` parks
plus Shenandoah's dedicated path — 19 of the 63 capital-P National Parks. This
pass researched the **remaining 44** against their **current (2025–2026)
superintendent's compendiums**, so all 63 National Parks now have a verified
gathering determination.

Status key: **verified** = read from the current primary nps.gov source this
pass; **carve-out** = the only gathering allowance is tribal/subsistence/cultural,
not a general-visitor right. Conservative default applies throughout: under
**36 CFR 2.1(a)** removing plants is prohibited unless the superintendent has
designated an exception under **36 CFR 2.1(c)** (or, for Alaska parklands,
**36 CFR 13.35(c)**). No designation found → prohibited.

All 44 findings were produced by a fan-out research run (one agent per park) and
then **independently adversarially re-verified** by a second agent biased toward
catching overstated permission. Two findings were corrected on verification
(American Samoa downgraded to prohibited; Great Sand Dunes limits corrected); one
mushroom flag (North Cascades) was hand-corrected during synthesis — see Notes.

## Notes, caveats, and conservative-default decisions

- **Alaska parklands use a different mechanism.** Denali, Gates of the Arctic,
  Glacier Bay, Katmai, Kobuk Valley, Lake Clark, and Wrangell–St. Elias allow
  general-visitor hand-gathering under **36 CFR 13.35(c)** (the Alaska
  parklands rule), which the compendiums defer to ("2.1(c) … Superseded by
  13.35(c)"). This is **separate from ANILCA Title VIII subsistence**, a right
  of federally qualified rural residents only — the encoded rule is the
  general-visitor allowance, and notes flag the distinction. Denali and Katmai
  designations are anchored to the **former monument/old-park core**; the 1980
  ANILCA additions and preserves carry separate subsistence rules.
- **Mushroom flag vs. safety whitelist.** 17 of the 34 parks legally designate
  edible fungi. Setting `mushroomsAllowed: true` for them only changes how
  *already-whitelisted* mushroom species are labeled in those parks; it does
  **not** add taxa and does not relax the project's rule of "no new fungi taxa
  without a species-level edible whitelist" (CLAUDE.md). Several compendiums use
  a blanket "edible fungi" phrase with no species list — the app's whitelist gate
  (`unlistedFungusRule` / `EDIBLE_FUNGUS_WHITELIST`) remains the operative safety
  control. The 17 mushroom-prohibited parks split into explicit bans (Black
  Canyon, Wind Cave) and simple non-designation (the rest); both code as
  `mushroomsAllowed: false`.
- **Cultural/tribal/subsistence carve-outs are recorded, not coded as
  permission.** Badlands (Lakota), Joshua Tree (chia seed), American Samoa
  (Samoan subsistence), Saguaro (Tohono Oʼodham saguaro fruit/cholla buds, a
  *separate* allowance layered on top of the general native-fruit designation),
  and Haleakalā/Hawaiʻi Volcanoes (Native Hawaiian practice; some non-natives by
  written permission). For general-visitor map semantics, carve-out-only parks
  are prohibited.
- **American Samoa was downgraded on verification.** The compendium carries the
  boilerplate 2.1(c) sentence but no populated species/quantity list; the only
  real allowance is traditional Samoan subsistence on village-leased land. This
  is exactly the false-positive the adversarial pass was built to catch — coded
  prohibited for general visitors.
- **Kenai Fjords** rests on the park's public "Collecting and Harvesting" page
  rather than a 2.1(c) compendium list (the 2025 compendium has none). Weaker
  provenance than the others but still a current primary nps.gov source; flagged
  in the entry note.
- **`match`-string collision fixes for encoding** (full guidance in the work
  order): use **`grand canyon national park`** (not `grand canyon`, which would
  catch Grand Canyon-Parashant NM); **`glacier bay`** never collides with the
  encoded `glacier national park`; **`wrangell`** is robust to hyphen/period
  variants in PAD-US `Unit_Nm`; keep the long forms `big bend national park`,
  `saguaro national park`, `great basin national park`, `theodore roosevelt
  national park`, `virgin islands national park`, `hot springs national park`,
  `zion national park`. Hawaiʻi unit names: PAD-US typically stores ASCII
  ("Haleakala", "Hawaii Volcanoes") so the ASCII matches work; if a feed uses the
  ʻokina/macron, the match needs adjusting — noted in the work order.

## Provenance

44 parks researched June 15 2026 via a fan-out workflow: one agent per park read
the park's current superintendent's compendium (many parsed directly from the
signed PDF with pypdf/PyMuPDF to avoid summarizer drift) and/or official rules
page on nps.gov, then a second, independent agent adversarially re-verified each
finding with a bias toward catching overstated permission. All 44 returned
**verified** status (read from a current primary source); 41 high-confidence, 3
medium (American Samoa, Black Canyon, Saguaro). Compendium dates span Jul 2022
(Virgin Islands — the only pre-2025 source) through Jun 2026.
