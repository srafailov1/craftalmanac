Cooked Elderberry Cordial

Cooked elderberry drink - never raw

  • Food map
  • Fresh
  • Beginner
  • Deep ruby-black cordial
  • Toxic — read safety
Safety — read before you start
  • Cook thoroughly - raw elderberries, juice, leaves, stems, and unripe berries contain cyanogenic glycosides that can cause nausea, vomiting, and cramping. Simmer 15-20 minutes.
  • Destem completely; never include green plant parts.
  • Do not substitute red elderberry (S. racemosa) - it is more toxic and requires deseeding plus cooking; most guidance is to avoid it entirely.
  • Occurrence on the map is not harvest permission - confirm you may legally collect where you forage.

About

The dark umbels of Sambucus canadensis carry a hard rule baked in: cook them, always. Raw berries and every green part hold cyanogenic glycosides that release cyanide during digestion, and hospital-documented cases trace back to raw juice. A hard simmer breaks those compounds down and leaves a cordial the color of a garnet held to candlelight.

Ingredients

  • Ripe dark blue/black elderberries (Sambucus nigra ssp. canadensis / European S. nigra), destemmed — 4 cups · Only fully ripe dark-blue/black berries. American elderberry (canadensis) is variable-acid. Do not use red elderberry (S. racemosa) in this recipe - its seeds are more toxic and require removal; treat it as an advanced, separate project only with reliable species-level guidance.
  • Water — 2 cups
  • Sugar — 1.5-2 cups · Adjust after juicing.
  • Lemon juice — 2 tbsp (optional) · Brightens flavor.
  • Ginger, cinnamon, cloves — to taste (optional) · Traditional warming spices.

Tools

Required: Large pot, Fine strainer or jelly bag, Fork or berry comb

Optional: Food mill, Immersion blender

Steps

  1. Comb every berry off the green stems - stems and unripe fruit are toxic. Discard red, green, or unripe berries.
  2. Simmer berries with water 15-20 minutes until fully collapsed. This cooking step is the safety step: it destroys cyanogenic glycosides.
  3. Mash and strain through a jelly bag or fine sieve; discard solids.
  4. Return juice to the pot, add sugar and lemon, simmer 5-10 minutes to dissolve and thicken slightly.
  5. Cool, bottle, and refrigerate. Dilute to taste with sparkling or still water.

Variations & modifiers

Add a cinnamon stick and star anise for a mulled winter cordial; a splash extracts beautifully into hot water as a tea base. Do not use red elderberry (Sambucus racemosa) here.

Preservation & storage

Refrigerate 2-4 weeks; freeze in jars or cubes for months. American elderberry (canadensis) is variable-acid and must never be water-bath canned without an NCHFP-tested recipe - do not water-bath an untested formula.

Yield

About 3-4 cups cordial from 4 cups berries. Expect real shrinkage; wild umbels are seedy.

Beyond the recipe

Strained pulp can be simmered again for a weaker second-run tea, then composted. The cordial base doubles as a topping for the cooked pawpaw ice cream or persimmon pudding in this collection.
