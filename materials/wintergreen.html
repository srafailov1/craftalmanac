Wintergreen berries

Gaultheria procumbens

Wintergreen, or eastern teaberry (Gaultheria procumbens), is a low evergreen woodland groundcover with aromatic red berries and the classic wintergreen scent. On this map it is a small, mostly fall-through-spring nibble and tea plant — used in tiny amounts because of its aspirin-like chemistry.

Identification & lookalikes

A short (4–6 in) evergreen groundcover with glossy, oval, alternate leaves that smell strongly of wintergreen when crushed, plus bright red berries that persist through winter. The scent is the key test: crush a leaf or berry — true wintergreen smells unmistakably of wintergreen. The similar-looking partridgeberry (Mitchella repens) has OPPOSITE, smooth, whitish-veined leaves, twin/double-eyed berries, and NO wintergreen fragrance (it's harmless but a different plant). Far more important, in the same woods watch for baneberry (Actaea) — a taller herb with compound leaves and clustered red or white berries that is DEADLY; wintergreen's low stature, simple aromatic leaves, and single berries distinguish it. This matches the entry's deadly-lookalike and salicylate tags.

Habitat & range

Native to acidic woodland floors, often under conifers and in mixed forests, across the northeastern and Great Lakes states and south in the Appalachians. Berries ripen in fall and persist through winter into spring, so they are gathered roughly September through April.

In the kitchen

The berries and leaves flavor teas, and the berries can be nibbled or added to preserves — always in small amounts. The honest limit is chemistry: wintergreen contains methyl salicylate, closely related to aspirin, which is concentrated further in distilled wintergreen oil (the oil is toxic in small volumes and must never be ingested). Reconciling with the entry's tags, eat only small amounts of the plant, and avoid it with aspirin/salicylate sensitivity or blood thinners, in pregnancy and breastfeeding, and for children.

Field note

Low evergreen woodland groundcover; crush a leaf or berry to confirm the strong wintergreen scent (separates it from scentless partridgeberry). Contains aspirin-like methyl salicylate — eat only small amounts and avoid with aspirin sensitivity or blood thinners, in pregnancy/breastfeeding, and in children. DEADLY lookalike: baneberry (Actaea), red berries in the same woods, separated by aroma, taller stature, and compound leaves.

Safety

Contains aspirin-like methyl salicylate — use small amounts of the plant only and never ingest wintergreen essential oil; avoid with blood thinners/aspirin sensitivity, in pregnancy, and for children. Confirm the wintergreen scent and avoid deadly baneberry.
